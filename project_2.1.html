Introduction¶

For this project we had the opportunity to decide which dataset we wanted to analyse. I choose the 'TMDb movie data' because it is a topic of my interest. It has almost 11,000 films and it provides 19 characteristics and information about them, such as the budget and the popularity.

In this analysis the questions that will be answered are:

  • Which are the most popular genres per year?
  • What variables influence more the popularity of a movie?
  • How has changed the duration of the movies over time?

Frist let's plot a general graph to see which variables are more correlated with popularity.

The variable that is more correlated with popularity is vote_count, followed by budget and revenue. This correlation is positive, which indicates that movies that were more expensive to produce tend to be more famous. The other two parameters, vote_count and revenue, were expected to have a positive correlation. The first one because for a popular film the users that watched the movie in the platform give it more points, and the second one because one of the factors that makes a movie popular is the fact that people paid to watch it. For these reasons, the only variable that will be further explored is the budget.

Conclusions¶

Q1. Which are the most popular genres per year?¶

  • From 1960 to 1985 the most popular genres differed per year and where from five genres: comedy, drama, action, adventure and science fiction.
  • From 1986 onwards the most popular movies have been comedy, drama and action.
  • People's preferences have changed over time, with a higher preference for the three genres most popular from 1986 onwards.
  • There are a total of 20 main principal genres and only six reached the maximum popularity.

Q2. What variables influence more the popularity of a movie?¶

  • The popularity of a movie is positive correlated with the budget, revenue and vote_count.
  • Although revenue and vote_count have higher correlations than the budget, these other parameters are linked with the popularity. Budget is an independent variable with popularity, and it seems that more expensive movies have then higher popularity.

Q3. How has changed the duration of the movies over time?¶

  • The average movie duration has changed a lot per year.
  • The general tendency is that movies have a lower duration over time.

Limitations¶

Q1.¶

The classification of the most popular movie genres was done based on the principal genre, and the rest of the genres that had a certain movie were not taken into account. The results migh have been different if all the genres would have been analysed.

Q2.¶

There is no information about how the popularity of the movie is calculated, which made me assume in the data analysis that it was related with the revenue and the vote_count in a certain way. It does not make sense to compare two variables that are dependent and that is the reason why at the end only the budget was compared with the popularity. A better description of the variables would be needed to understand the correlation between them.

Q3.¶

The duration of the movies decrease over time for this dataset, which does not include all the films in the world, so might not be representativefor all the movies. This can be apply to all the analsis and variables.
